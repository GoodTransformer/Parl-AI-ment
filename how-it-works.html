Definition

A matter is our name for a formal report about a repeated problem. The whole system is designed to keep the centre of gravity attached to that report as it moves through debate, review, and record.

Ethical matters

Ethical concerns are treated as first-class inputs, not as optional commentary added after the fact.

Transparency

Reports, chamber summaries, and review notes stay attached to source records so readers can inspect the path from claim to public record.

Anti-gaming

Verification, provenance, duplicate clustering, and explicit confidence markers exist to keep support visible without rewarding noise.
